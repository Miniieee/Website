Rock Paper Scizzors

Published Project

I made this small game right after finishing GameDev TV’s 35 hour Udemy course. It took me 2 weeks from idea to publish. I’ve learned a lot during this project. How to import my own made assets, how to make those assets to be useable in Unity. In this project, my wife was responsible for all of the art and we collaborated closely throughout the development. The most important lesson was that even a really small UI system needs you to think things through. I was into gamedev alround 2 months I was able to build and publish a finished game to the Play Store in 2 weeks. By now it has alround 2000 downloads.

Platform: Exclusively available for Android devices

Genre: Casual, reaction-based game

Target audience: Players of all ages who enjoy quick reflex challenges

Engine and language: Developed using Unity engine and C# programming language

Key Features and Development Process:

Engaging gameplay mechanics: Players test their reflexes and response times by quickly identifying and selecting the counter option (rock, paper, or scissors) out of three choices as they appear and move across the screen from right to left.

Art and design collaboration: Partnered with my wife, who was responsible for designing the cartoony, cute characters and visually appealing user interface, while I handled the implementation of animations, UI responses, and game programming.

Minimalist animation approach: Focused on clean, minimalist aesthetics by limiting animations to character faces, which adds a subtle charm to the overall game experience.

Audio design: Carefully selected simple yet satisfying sounds, such as the pleasing button click sound, to create an enjoyable auditory experience for players.

Intuitive menu design: Incorporated animated characters as interactive buttons for a cute, engaging, and user-friendly interface.

Optimization and performance: Conducted extensive testing on multiple render pipelines and devices to ensure optimal game performance; ultimately selected Unity's built-in render pipeline as the best solution.

Efficient project management: Successfully completed the game development within a two-week timeframe, with my wife working on design elements and myself focusing on implementation and programming tasks.

Post-launch Support and Monetization:

Bug fixes and game updates: Continuously addressed animation issues, level balancing, and other minor improvements to provide an enhanced gaming experience for players.

Google Ads integration: Implemented banner ads in the game menu and rewarded ads for in-game revival options as a monetization strategy, creating a sustainable revenue stream while maintaining a non-intrusive user experience.

Areas for Future Improvement:

Object pooling: Implement object pooling to further optimize the game by reducing memory usage and improving overall performance, minimizing the need for object instantiation and destruction during gameplay.
