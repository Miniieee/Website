Abduction Chronicles: The Farm

In development

Platform: Will be available on Steam for most PC VR headsetss

Genre: Horror

Engine and language: Developed using Unity engine and C# programming language

Key Features and Development Process:

Immersive narrative: Working on creating an engaging story set on a remote country farmhouse, where players must uncover the mystery of their brother's disappearance and unravel the strange occurrences surrounding the farm.

First-person VR gameplay mechanics: Developing interactive and realistic gameplay using VR capabilities, with players wielding a flashlight and navigating through a power outage using limited light sources.

Event-driven progression: Designing story advancement through trigger boxes and item interactions that initiate specific events and plot revelations.

In-game communication: Incorporating conversations with the protagonist's wife as a gameplay element, offering guidance and plot exposition to help players progress.

Semi-realistic art style : Striving to achieve a balance between realism and resource optimization, using the game's low-light setting to creatively handle distant objects and environmental details.

Immersive audio design: Emphasizing the importance of audio in creating a suspenseful and terrifying atmosphere, planning to employ fine-tuning and high-quality sound effects to maximize the horror experience.

Minimalist UI: Developing a clean, unobtrusive user interface that complements the immersive nature of the VR gameplay.

Project Timeline and Post-launch Support:

Occlusion culling: Employ occlusion culling techniques to optimize rendering performance and maintain a smooth VR experience.

Static batching: Utilize static batching to improve the game's rendering efficiency, reducing draw calls and optimizing resource usage.

GPU instancing: Aiming to implement GPU instancing to further enhance rendering performance and maintain high-quality graphics for VR gameplay.

Quality settings fine-tuning: Anticipating the need to continuously refine quality settings to strike a balance between visual fidelity and performance, ensuring an optimal VR experience for players.
